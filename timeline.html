TIMELINE TECHNOLOGY CONSEQUENCES INTERVIEW
➤
1958 1967 1968 1972 1974 1982 1985 1986 1993 2005 2006
1958
a former marine invents the side-handled baton, which is more effective for many officers than the original baton, which was basically a club
1967
The Presidents Commission on Law Enforcement and Administrative Justice finds that while many police departments have made good use of their new-found technology, most of it could have been adopted about 30 years earlier.
1968
911 invented. In the years following it spreads into universal use.
1972
The National Institute of Justice begins development of a lightweight body armor made from kevlar.
1974
the TASER is developed in its first incarnation, but is classified as a firearm due to its use of gunpowder in 1976.
1982
Pepper spray, also known as OC spray after the compound it is composed of, is developed.
1985
Police in every state are using TASERs, a useful non-lethal weapon if used correctly.
1986
DNA evidence used to prove that Richard Buckland didn’t commit two murders, and is later used to prove that the murders were committed by Colin Pitchfork.
1993
About 90% of large police departments use computers.
2005
First use of the LRAD, a device used to disperse crowds and to provide a means to speak over several communicators is first used to foiled a pirate attack on a cruise ship off the Somali coast.
2006
The ShotSpotter gunshot locater is first installed. It is done so in Oakland, and provided local police with ability to precisely locate gunshots, as well as the ability to get to start rushing to the scene the instant the shot is fired.
➤

Ever since ancient times, governments have needed to police their citizens. There have been many years years of law enforcement—protecting and serving America and the world. Being a police officer is one of the harder and more dangerous jobs people can have. Consequently, police agencies have looked for ways to minimize the danger and increase the success of police officers, and advanced technology is a key way to do this.
